CHAPTER ONE — Don’t Lie to the Map Nobody claps when you decide to stop running. There’s no parade for quitting the thing that was slowly killing you. No trophy for choosing clarity over chaos. Most of the time, the only sound you hear is the static in your own head and the quiet judgment of people who don’t know half the shit you survived. And even the ones who do know? A lot of them still don’t clap. They just watch. Waiting to see if this is real — or just another chapter you’ll pretend never happened. That’s life. That’s people. That’s exactly why this decision has to be ten toes. This chapter isn’t about motivation. It’s about orientation. Because before you build anything — a life, a future, a business, an empire, even a peaceful morning — you have to find the Idiolocator. That sign in the mall that says: YOU ARE HERE. In recovery, it hits different. It doesn’t say: “You’re doing great.” “You’re almost there.” “You’re special.” It says: You are here. In rehab. Rebuilding. Stripped of shortcuts. No substances. No lies that still work. That dot is humbling as hell. That’s why most people refuse to stand on it. They want to skip ahead. They want to jump straight to the comeback story. They want to talk goals before they admit reality. But the map doesn’t move for fantasies. You don’t get to route yourself out of chaos if you’re lying about where you’re standing. And this is where the Baltimore in me speaks up — not loud, not dramatic, just calm and serious: You can’t fake your location and still make it home. To be continued…
